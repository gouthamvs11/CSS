NIKE BLAZER
The Nike Blazer, introduced in 1973 as a basketball shoe, is an iconic and enduring Nike silhouette known for its clean, classic design with a large Swoosh logo. Available in high- and low-top versions, its features include a leather upper, vulcanized construction, and a herringbone-patterned rubber outsole for durability and traction. Named after Nike's home team, the Portland Trail Blazers, the Blazer's robust construction and vintage styling have made it a versatile staple, transitioning from basketball performance to everyday streetwear and skateboarding.
